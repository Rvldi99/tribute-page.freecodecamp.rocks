Wahyu Sardono (Dono)

Comedian Actor and lecturer of sociology

Here's a time line of Wahyu Sardono's life:

  • 30 September 1951

    Born in Delanggu, Klaten, Central Java, Indonesia.

  • Late 1960 - Early 1970s

    Moves to Jakarta for higher education.

    Studies Sociology at the University of Indonesia (UI) — and later becomes a lecturer there.

  • 1973

    Joins the Warung Kopi Prambors radio comedy show, which would later evolve into Warkop (short for Warung Kopi).

  • 1979

    Warkop transitions to the big screen with the film "Mana Tahaaan..." — launching a long and successful movie career.

    Dono, along with Kasino and Indro, becomes a household name in Indonesian comedy.

  • 1980 - 1990s

    Stars in a series of hit Warkop DKI films, such as:

    • Gengsi Dong (1980)
    • Maju Kena Mundur Kena (1983)
    • CHIPS (1982)
    • Pintar-Pintar Bodoh (1980)

    The trio's unique humor combines slapstick, social satire, and clever wordplay, helping define modern Indonesian comedy.

  • 1990s

    Even as Warkop's movie era slows, Dono continues to write, act, and entertain, maintaining his status as a national comedy icon.

  • 30 November 2001

    Dono passes away at age 50 in Jakarta after battling lymph node cancer.

    His death marks the end of an era for Indonesian comedy fans.

"Time is the best gift, when a person gives his time, he gives a part of his life that cannot be taken back."
- Dono Warkop
